Earth

The only planet we know of that harbors life, which began in the oceans. The Earth has a powerful magnetic field, caused by the elements its core is made out of. Thanks to it, we are protected from deadly Sun phenomena, such as solar winds. It is the only planet in our solar system that isn't named after a god.

Mars

Mars is home to the tallest mountain in our solar system - a volcano spanning 21km in height and 600km in diameter, making it bigger than some countries. Less than half of humanity's missions to this planet have been successful: only 26 of the 56 total. We have found pieces of it on our own Earth though, which has allowed us to study it before launching space missions to it.

Venus

A day on this planet lasts longer than a year: it takes 243 Earth days to complete a rotation around its axis, while taking only 225 Earth days to complete an orbit around the Sun. As the hottest planet in our solar system, its sufrace temperature sits at an astonishing 462 ° Celcius.

Jupiter

This gas giant is so massive, it has a storm called "The Great Red Spot" (lower half of picture) which can fit 3 Earth. It has been raging on for at least 350 years.Although it might not look like it at a first glance, Juputer does have a ring system, which begins about 92,000km above its cloud tops, and stretches out more than 225,000km from the planet.

Saturn

The most distant planet that can be seen with the naked eye. It has oval-shaped storms similar to Jupiter's. Obviously, Saturn has the most extensive (and beautiful) rings of any other planet in our solar system. They stretch more than 120,000km from the planet, but are unbelievably thin on a space scale: only 20 meters thick. It is the planet with the most moons in the solar system.

Space has been the subject of study for millenia. Find out and learn more below.

Black Holes

Gigantic objects that we can't see directly, since not even light can escape them. We study them by observing their surroundings. For example, a star that's close enough to a black hole can be seen being ripped apart.

Aliens

There is a paradox in the astronomy world: We have estimated that there are millions upon millions of stars, each with its planets around it, just like our Sun. Why haven't we found life on a single one yet?

Quasars

An extremely luminous active galactic nucleus, in which a supermassive black hole with mass ranging from millions to billions of times the mass of the Sun is surrounded by a gaseous accretion disk.

Spacecraft

The most distant human-made object from us is Voyager 1. It is over 13 billion miles away from Earth. It was launched in 1977, along with its twin, Voyager 2. Both of them flew by Jupiter and Saturn.
